Der Klassiker is German football’s pressure cooker, and this Allianz Arena meeting refused to cool. Bayern Munich defeated Borussia Dortmund 3-2 in a match that looked like a tactical seminar one minute and a track meet the next.

Harry Kane opened the scoring in the ninth minute from a rehearsed free-kick routine. Joshua Kimmich delivered flat to the near post, Kane peeled off his marker, and the finish was pure centre-forward instinct. Dortmund’s reply arrived through pace. Karim Adeyemi’s 24th-minute equaliser punished a Bayern turnover high up the pitch — one ball behind the line, one sprint, one clinical finish.

## Midfield control and Musiala’s influence

Bayern’s second-half plan was clearer: pin Dortmund’s wing-backs, flood the half-spaces, and let Jamal Musiala invent. The 51st-minute goal was Musiala at his best — receiving between lines, attracting two markers, and slipping a finish after a one-two with Kane.

Dortmund refused to fold. Julian Brandt’s 67th-minute strike from the edge of the box made it 2-2 after a messy second-ball situation. For a spell, Nuri Şahin’s side looked the more dangerous on the break, especially whenever Adeyemi isolated Bayern’s last defender.

## Olise decides it late

The winner belonged to Michael Olise. In the 84th minute, Bayern overloaded the right, Kimmich delayed the cross, and Olise arrived at the edge of the area to strike low through a crowd. It was the kind of late quality that separates title favourites from nearly sides.

Possession finished 57-43 to Bayern, with a 21-12 shot advantage. MatchVault’s Bundesliga desk notes that Dortmund were competitive in transitions but conceded too many controlled entries into the box. In Der Klassiker, surviving waves is not enough — you must stop the next one.
