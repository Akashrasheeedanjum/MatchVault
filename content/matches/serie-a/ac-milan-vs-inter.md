Few fixtures compress tactics and emotion like the Derby della Madonnina. On a February night at San Siro, Inter Milan left with a 2-1 victory built on patience without the ball and ruthlessness with it.

Simone Inzaghi’s Inter started in a flexible 3-5-2 that became a 5-3-2 out of possession. The aim was clear: deny AC Milan’s central progressions and force the game toward the flanks, where Inter’s wing-backs could duel and reset. Marcus Thuram’s opener on 27 minutes rewarded that plan. A Milan buildup pass was intercepted, Nicolò Barella drove forward, and Thuram finished first time across Mike Maignan.

## Milan’s equaliser and the Leão problem

AC Milan needed a spark and found it in Rafael Leão. The equaliser on 58 minutes was individual excellence more than structural dominance — Leão collected on the left, drove inside, and bent a shot beyond Yann Sommer. For fifteen minutes Milan’s crowd believed the derby had flipped.

Inter’s coaching staff responded by narrowing the midfield distances and asking Barella to shadow the pocket Leão preferred. Milan kept more of the ball (54%), but the quality of entries into the box dropped. Crosses became hopeful; cut-backs were blocked.

## Lautaro closes the derby

Lautaro Martínez wrote the final line on 76 minutes. A recycled corner dropped at the edge of the area, Hakan Çalhanoğlu disguised a pass, and Lautaro spun his marker before finishing low. Inter’s celebrations said everything about derby hierarchy in that moment.

Shots finished 11-14 in Inter’s favour despite less possession — a classic Inzaghi signature. MatchVault’s Serie A analysis: Milan were brave in buildup but predictable once Inter closed the half-spaces. In Milan derbies, the team that wins the second balls usually wins the night. Inter won both.
